Client Report

When I was designing my site plan and wireframe for my webpage, I wanted it to be about something I cared about. This led me to the topic it is about which is natural landmarks and monuments. I was able to make sure my developers knew this even wihtout being versed in the topic through my site plan. This had all the photos and descriptions they needed for the site, so they could just focus on the html and css. The developers of my site did very well. They followed the wireframe I made and used colors appropriately, making the end product way better than I could've ever imagined. Next time I require developers for a site I will be willing to be more detailed with my instructions, site plan, and wireframe.

Lead Developer Report

As the lead developer, it was my job to keep the creation of the site on track and communicate with both the junior developer and the client. Staying in constant communication was key, as it allowed the client to make corrections and suggestions to the site quickly and allowed me to hand out work to the junior developer to make the creation of the site go faster. We all communicated through email and text, allowing us to send files to each other and get ahold of each other quickly. I was able to publish the site through Github easily and share the link from github to the client, allowing them to check up on progess continually.

Junior Developer Report

As the junior developer, it was my role to carryout any tasks the lead developer needed of me and to create and style the child pages. To best do this, we communicated through Microsoft Teams, allowing the sending of messages and files. What was expected of me was well defined at the beginning of the project. Being able to assist the lead developer was a secondary but crucial role. If the lead developer was ever busy or behind, I was able to assist him and keep us on schedule. I ran into no issues with Github or VSCode during this project.
